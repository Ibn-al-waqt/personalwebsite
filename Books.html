Books

The 48 Laws of Power

This books consists of 48 laws of power that will help you rise the ranks while avoiding trouble. Many examples from time periods such as Three Kingdoms China, Industrial Age America, Rennaisance Italy, and revolutionary France serve as stories with the purpose of illustrating the points mentioned in the book. Power can make or break one's future and this is the guidebook for it.





The Alchemist

In this book, a young shepherd from Iberia(modern day Spain) sets out to seek his fortune, which he saw in his dream about the Great Pyramids. Along the way, he meets many elder figures who guide him towards his journey despite the unknown obstacles. He connects with the universal spirit, which might be the key to achieving his journey







Percy Jackson and the Lightning Thief

Percy Jackson is a teenager living with his mom and playing with his best friend Glover. However, some monsters show up and after going to Camp Half-Blood, he learns that the Greek gods and mythology are real. He is suspected of stealing Thunder God Zeus's lightning bolt and he must undertake a quest to prove his innoncence.







Physics of the Impossible

Michio Kaku talks about how some future technologies that are seen as pure fiction could actually become realities through our understanding of physics. According to him, invisibility, force fields, teleportation, and starships are inventions of the future and that we only need to develop them. What would such technologies look like and where are we now?






What If?

Randall Munroe takes some common, unusual, or hilarious questions and answers them using science and handdrawings. From powering houses using lava moats to throwing baseballs at 90% the speed of light, Randall is a master at communicating science concepts in a fun and intriguing way. A must-read if you want to know more about science
